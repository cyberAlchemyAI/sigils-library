# Arcana - Sovereign Sigils

Arcana are autonomous behavior sigils: recursive, multi-turn, or multi-agent frameworks that manage uncertainty across goals, evidence, roles, and governance gates.

They are used when a single bounded transformation is not enough. Arcana coordinate inquiry, split concerns, synthesize tensions, preserve session state, and route decisions back through human validation when the stakes or ambiguity justify it.

## Epistemic Contract

- Primary logic: strategic orchestration and recursive reasoning.
- State model: persistent across phases, agents, or human gates.
- Input tolerance: high, but ambiguity must be surfaced and governed.
- Output shape: synthesized findings, decisions, action routes, and preserved context.
- Success measure: emergent insight, alignment, and responsible continuation.

## Use Arcana For

- multi-agent investigation,
- cross-layer tension discovery,
- research orchestration,
- long-running governance loops,
- workflows where the next step depends on synthesized evidence and human validation.

## Avoid Arcana When

- a local deterministic check would answer the question,
- a single artifact synthesis is enough,
- the user wants immediate implementation of a well-scoped change,
- the workflow lacks a human gate for consequential interpretation.

## Quality Bar

An Arcana sigil is ready when:

- it clearly states when its coordination cost is justified,
- it defines roles, phases, gates, and preservation rules,
- it prevents action before synthesis and validation,
- it distinguishes findings, tensions, decisions, and follow-up work,
- it can recover or stop cleanly when evidence is insufficient.

## Relationship To Other Tiers

- Arcana can delegate Formulae checks for deterministic validation.
- Arcana can delegate Transmutations for bounded synthesis inside a larger workflow.
- Arcana should not replace small direct work when the task does not need orchestration.
